\opening{Dear Editor,}

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled
\textbf{``Secluded Majorana Dark Matter with a Light Scalar Mediator:
Velocity-Dependent Self-Interactions from Yukawa Potentials''}
for consideration as a Regular Article in \textit{Physical Review~D}.

This work constructs a minimal secluded dark-matter model in which a
Majorana fermion interacts with a light scalar mediator via a
mixed (scalar + pseudoscalar) Yukawa coupling.  The self-interaction
cross section is computed non-perturbatively using the Variable Phase
Method, which captures quasi-bound-state resonances of the Yukawa
potential.

The principal results are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item A systematic scan over $(m_\chi, m_\phi, \alpha_s)$ identifies
        \textbf{80{,}142 parameter points} satisfying the SIDM
        sweet-spot criteria on both dwarf and cluster scales.

  \item An exact numerical Boltzmann solver pins down 17 benchmark
        points that simultaneously satisfy the thermal relic density
        $\Omega h^2 = 0.120$, SIDM sweet-spot cuts, and cluster
        constraints---forming an ``island of viability'' at
        $m_\chi \in [10, 100]$~GeV, $m_\phi \in [7.6, 14.8]$~MeV.

  \item A quantitative $\chi^2$ fit to 13 astrophysical systems
        spanning $v = 12$--$4700$~km/s yields
        $\chi^2/\nu = 0.38$ for the best relic-constrained point,
        confirmed by Bayesian MCMC posterior analysis.

  \item A CP-channel decomposition reveals a unique Majorana
        quantum-statistics signature: the triplet (CP-odd) scattering
        fraction peaks at 82\% near $v \approx 60$~km/s, producing a
        Majorana-to-Dirac cross section ratio of 2.2 at precisely the
        rotation-curve velocity scale.  This represents a falsifiable
        prediction for next-generation kinematic measurements.

  \item Three independent external validations---the Kaplinghat--Tulin--Yu
        nine-system compilation, Fornax globular-cluster timing, and
        Oman et al.\ halo diversity---are passed without post-hoc
        parameter tuning.
\end{itemize}

The manuscript presents nine concrete falsifiability criteria and
demonstrates that a Higgs portal to the Standard Model is generically
excluded for light mediators, making a secluded dark sector the
natural resolution.

The full numerical code and data are publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/0mp3s/Secluded-Majorana-SIDM}.
This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and all results are
original.

We believe this work is well suited for \textit{Physical Review~D}
given its scope in particle dark-matter phenomenology and its
quantitative connection to astrophysical observations.
