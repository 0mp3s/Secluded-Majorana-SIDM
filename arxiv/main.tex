\begin{abstract}
We construct a minimal secluded dark matter model in which a Majorana
fermion~$\chi$ couples to a light real scalar~$\phi$ through a
mixed Yukawa interaction
$-(1/2)\bar\chi(y_s+iy_p\gamma_5)\chi\,\phi$.
The dark sector has no
tree-level coupling to the Standard Model.  The $\phi$-mediated
self-interaction produces velocity-dependent scattering governed by the
attractive Yukawa potential $V(r)=-\alpha_s\,e^{-\mphi r}/r$ with
$\alpha_s=y_s^{2}/(4\pi)$.

We compute the self-interaction transfer cross section using the
\emph{Variable Phase Method}~(VPM)---a full partial-wave analysis that
captures quasi-bound-state resonances of the Yukawa potential.  A
systematic scan over $(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$---with
$\mchi\in[0.1,100]\GeV$, $\mphi\in[0.1,200]\MeV$---identifies
\textbf{80,142} parameter points satisfying the SIDM
sweet-spot criteria
$\sigmam(30\kms)\in[1,10]\cmg$ and
$\sigmam(1000\kms)<0.47\cmg$.

The annihilation $\chi\chi\to\phi\phi$ proceeds via an \emph{s-wave}
channel enabled by a mixed scalar--pseudoscalar Yukawa coupling
$(y_s + iy_p\gamma_5)$ that breaks CP in the dark sector, yielding a
thermal relic density $\OmH=0.120$ for
$\alpha_s\sim 5\times10^{-4}\text{--}5\times10^{-3}$---squarely within
the SIDM-viable region.  A dedicated cosmological scan using an exact
numerical Boltzmann solver identifies \textbf{17 benchmark
points} forming an ``island of viability'' at
$\mchi\in[10,100]\GeV$, $\mphi\in[7.6,14.8]\MeV$ that simultaneously
satisfy relic density, SIDM, and cluster constraints.  A quantitative
$\chi^{2}$ fit to 13 astrophysical systems spanning $v=12$--$4700\kms$
yields $\chi^{2}/\nu=0.38$ for the best relic-constrained
point.  A Bayesian MCMC posterior analysis confirms that all
17 relic benchmarks lie within the 95\% credible region.
A CP-channel decomposition reveals that the triplet (CP-odd) scattering
fraction peaks at 82\% near $v\approx 60\kms$---a unique Majorana
signature producing $\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}/\sigma_T^{\rm Dir}=2.2$ at
precisely the rotation-curve velocity scale.  We
show that a Higgs portal coupling to the SM is generically
\emph{excluded} for light mediators
($\mphi\lesssim\mathcal{O}(\text{GeV})$) by the tension between direct
detection and BBN lifetime constraints; a secluded dark sector is the
natural resolution.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The standard cold dark matter (CDM) paradigm, while remarkably
successful at explaining large-scale structure, faces persistent
challenges at galactic and sub-galactic scales.  These include the
core--cusp problem~\cite{deBlok:2009sp,Oh:2015xoa}, the missing
satellites problem~\cite{Klypin:1999uc}, the too-big-to-fail
problem~\cite{BoylanKolchin:2011de}, and the diversity of rotation
curves~\cite{Oman:2015xda}.  Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM),
first proposed by Spergel \& Steinhardt~\cite{Spergel:1999mh}, offers
a compelling resolution: DM particles scatter off each other at short
range, thermalizing the inner halo and producing flat density cores.

The key phenomenological requirement is velocity-dependent
self-interaction:
\begin{align}
  \sigmam &\sim 1\text{--}10\cmg \quad\text{at }v\sim 30\kms
    \;\text{(dwarfs)}\,,\\
  \sigmam &\lesssim 0.1\cmg \quad\text{at }v\sim 1000\kms
    \;\text{(clusters)}\,,
\end{align}
following~\cite{Clowe:2006eq,Robertson:2016xjh}.  This velocity
dependence arises naturally from Yukawa-type interactions mediated by a
light boson~\cite{Tulin:2013teo,Tulin:2017ara}.

In this work we consider a \textbf{secluded dark sector} containing a
Majorana fermion~$\chi$ coupled to a real scalar mediator~$\phi$ via a
mixed Yukawa interaction
$-(1/2)\bar\chi(y_s+iy_p\gamma_5)\chi\,\phi$.  We show that the
commonly invoked Higgs portal connection to the SM
($\lambda_{H\phi}|H|^{2}\phi^{2}$) is incompatible with light
mediators due to the $1/\mphi^{4}$ enhancement of the direct detection
cross section (\S\ref{sec:higgs_portal}), and instead adopt a secluded
framework with no direct SM coupling.  This minimal setup has several
attractive features:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The DM--DM self-interaction is an attractive Yukawa potential
  $V(r)=-\alpha_s\,e^{-\mphi r}/r$, supporting velocity-dependent
  resonant scattering.
\item The annihilation $\chi\chi\to\phi\phi$ is \emph{s-wave} thanks
  to the mixed scalar--pseudoscalar coupling, enabling a correct
  thermal relic density with couplings in the SIDM-viable range.
\item The secluded dark sector has $\siSI=0$---no tension with direct
  detection experiments---and annihilation products remain dark,
  evading CMB energy-injection bounds.
\item The model is minimal and anomaly-free, with self-interactions
  depending on only three parameters
  $(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$; the relic density fixes the
  product $\alpha_s\alpha_p$ as an additional one-sided constraint.
\end{enumerate}

We compute the self-interaction cross sections using the
\emph{Variable Phase Method}~(VPM)---a full partial-wave analysis that
correctly captures resonance structure near quasi-bound states of the
Yukawa potential.

\textbf{Related work.}  Light-mediator SIDM was introduced by Feng,
Kaplinghat, Tu \& Yu~\cite{Feng:2009mn} in the context of hidden
charged dark matter, and developed systematically by Tulin, Yu \&
Zurek~\cite{Tulin:2013teo} who provided fitting formulas for the
Yukawa transfer cross section.  The comprehensive review by Tulin \&
Yu~\cite{Tulin:2017ara} covers the full landscape of SIDM models.
Yang~\cite{Yang:2025abc} recently studied nearly degenerate Majorana DM
self-interactions in a gauged $U(1)_{L_\mu-L_\tau}$ model, which
differs from our approach in using a complex dark Higgs (8 parameters)
rather than a minimal real scalar (3 parameters), and relies on $p$-wave
annihilation for the relic density.  Our work differs from these
precedents in several respects: (i)~we use the full VPM partial-wave
analysis rather than approximate Hulth\'{e}n-potential fitting formulas;
(ii)~we demonstrate quantitatively that the Higgs portal is generically
excluded for light mediators; (iii)~we perform a combined SIDM + relic
density scan with an exact Boltzmann solver, identifying a well-defined
island of viability; and (iv)~we provide a quantitative~$\chi^{2}$ fit
to 13 astrophysical systems spanning four decades in velocity.

\section{The Model}\label{sec:model}

We consider a simplified dark sector consisting of a Majorana fermion
dark matter candidate~$\chi$ interacting via a real scalar
mediator~$\phi$.  The most general renormalizable Yukawa
interaction is~\cite{Kahlhoefer:2017umn}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lagrangian}
  \mathcal{L}_{\rm int}
  = -\frac{1}{2}\bar\chi\bigl(y_s + i\,y_p\,\gamma_5\bigr)\chi\,\phi\,,
\end{equation}
where $y_s$ and $y_p$ are real scalar and pseudoscalar couplings.
Both couplings are required: a pure-scalar coupling ($y_p=0$)
yields \emph{p-wave--suppressed} annihilation $\chi\chi\to\phi\phi$
for identical Majorana fermions, because the ${}^{1}S_{0}$ initial
state has $CP=-1$ while the two-boson $s$-wave final state has
$CP=+1$.  The simultaneous presence of $y_s\neq 0$ and $y_p\neq 0$
breaks CP in the dark sector and restores the $s$-wave
(see~\S\ref{sec:ann_xs}).

In the non-relativistic limit, the scalar coupling generates a
Yukawa potential between the dark matter particles:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:yukawa}
  V(r) = -\frac{\alpha_s}{r}\,e^{-\mphi r}\,,
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_s = y_s^{2}/(4\pi)$.  The pseudoscalar contribution
to the potential is suppressed by $(\mphi/\mchi)^{2}\sim 10^{-7}$
and is therefore negligible for elastic scattering (verified
numerically to $\Delta\sigma_T/\sigma_T < 10^{-6}$).
Because $\chi$ is a Majorana fermion
(identical to its own antiparticle), the DM--DM scattering amplitude
must be antisymmetrized, leading to non-trivial spin-weighted
partial-wave sums (even-$\ell$ singlet weight~1, odd-$\ell$ triplet
weight~3) and an overall identical-particle factor of~$1/2$.

A natural connection between $\phi$ and the SM would be the Higgs
portal coupling $\lambda_{H\phi}|H|^{2}\phi^{2}$.  We show in
\S\ref{sec:higgs_portal} that this is \textbf{generically excluded}
for light mediators ($\mphi\lesssim\mathcal{O}(\text{GeV})$): the
$1/\mphi^{4}$ enhancement of $\siSI$ closes the window between direct
detection and BBN bounds.  The dark sector is therefore
\textbf{secluded}---$\phi$ has no tree-level coupling to the SM.

\subsection{Parameters}\label{sec:params}

The model is specified by five Lagrangian parameters:

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Parameter & Range & Physical meaning \\
\midrule
$\mchi$ & 10--100\GeV & DM mass \\
$\mphi$ & 1--50\MeV & Mediator mass \\
$\alpha_s = y_s^{2}/(4\pi)$ & $10^{-5}$--$5\times10^{-2}$ & Scalar dark fine-structure constant \\
$\alpha_p = y_p^{2}/(4\pi)$ & --- & Pseudoscalar coupling \\
$\mu_{3}$ & $\gtrsim 1.7\,\mphi$ & Cubic self-coupling (\S\ref{sec:neff}) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Crucially, the three sectors decouple:
(i)~self-interactions depend only on $(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$;
(ii)~the relic density depends on $\alpha_s\alpha_p$ through the
$s$-wave annihilation cross section (\S\ref{sec:ann_xs});
(iii)~mediator cosmology depends on $\mu_3$ alone
(\S\ref{sec:neff}).  This makes the SIDM phenomenology a
three-parameter problem, with $\alpha_p$ and $\mu_3$ entering only as
one-sided constraints.  The model contains no free parameters related
to the SM--dark sector coupling.

\section{Self-Interaction Cross Section}\label{sec:sigma}

\subsection{Variable Phase Method}\label{sec:vpm}

The DM--DM scattering cross section via $\phi$ exchange is governed by
the attractive Yukawa potential of Eq.~\eqref{eq:yukawa}.  We compute
the transfer cross section using the Variable Phase Method (VPM), which
solves for the phase shifts~$\delta_\ell$ via the ODE~\cite{Hulthen:1942}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:vpm_ode}
  \frac{d\delta_\ell}{dx}
  = +\frac{\lam\,e^{-x}}{\kappa\,x}
    \bigl[\hat\jmath_\ell(\kappa x)\cos\delta_\ell
          -\hat n_\ell(\kappa x)\sin\delta_\ell\bigr]^{2}\,,
\end{equation}
where $x=\mphi r$ (dimensionless radial coordinate),
$\kappa=k/\mphi=\mu\vrel/\mphi$ with $\mu=\mchi/2$ the reduced mass,
$\lam=\alpha_s\mchi/\mphi$ (coupling strength parameter), and
$\hat\jmath_\ell,\hat n_\ell$ are Riccati--Bessel functions.  The
positive sign corresponds to an \emph{attractive} potential.

\subsection{Cross section for identical Majorana fermions}
\label{sec:majorana_xs}

For identical Majorana fermions, quantum statistics modifies the
partial-wave sum:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma_T}
  \sigma_T = \frac{2\pi}{k^{2}}\biggl[
    \sum_{\ell\;\text{even}}(2\ell+1)\sin^{2}\delta_\ell
    + 3\sum_{\ell\;\text{odd}}(2\ell+1)\sin^{2}\delta_\ell
  \biggr]\,.
\end{equation}
The factor of~3 for odd partial waves arises from spin statistics: the
Majorana fermion has spin-1/2, and the triplet spin state (weight~3)
combines with odd orbital angular momentum to form the antisymmetric
total wave function.  The factor $2\pi/k^{2}$ (rather than
$4\pi/k^{2}$) includes the conventional factor of~$1/2$ for
identical-particle scattering rates.

For identical Majorana fermions, the elastic and momentum-transfer
cross sections are \emph{identically equal}:
$\sigma_T^{\rm tr}\equiv\sigma_{\rm el}$.  The proof follows from the
parity of the partial-wave sum in each spin channel (Appendix~\ref{app:elastic_transfer}).

\subsection{Numerical implementation}\label{sec:numerics}

The VPM ODE is integrated using a 4th-order Runge--Kutta scheme with:
adaptive $x_{\rm max}$ (50--100), adaptive step count (4000--12000),
centrifugal barrier $x_{\rm min}=\max(10^{-5},\ell/\kappa)$ for
$\ell>0$, and early termination when the contribution falls below
$10^{-4}$ of the running peak.  The implementation is verified
against: (i)~free particle ($\delta_\ell(\lam=0)=0$ for all~$\ell$),
(ii)~Born limit agreement to $<0.1\%$ at weak coupling, and
(iii)~\texttt{scipy.integrate.solve\_ivp} (RK45, rtol=$10^{-10}$)
agreement to $<0.001\%$.

\subsection{Numerical error budget}\label{sec:error_budget}

A systematic error analysis (Appendix~\ref{app:error_budget})
identifies three components.  At the primary SIDM velocity
(30\kms, dwarf galaxies), the total systematic is negligible
($<0.01\%$) for all relic-constrained benchmarks.  At cluster scales
($v\sim 1000\kms$), the 10.8--13.6\% systematic for relic BPs is
dominated by the $x_{\rm min}$ prescription.

\section{Secluded Dark Sector and Direct Detection}\label{sec:secluded}

\subsection{Incompatibility of the Higgs portal with light mediators}
\label{sec:higgs_portal}

The most economical connection between $\phi$ and the SM is the Higgs
portal $\lambda_{H\phi}|H|^{2}\phi^{2}$.  The mixing angle
$\sin\theta$ mediates DM--nucleon scattering with:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma_si}
  \siSI = \frac{y^{2}\sin^{2}\theta\,f_{N}^{2}\,\mu_{N}^{2}\,m_{N}^{2}}
               {\pi\,v_{\rm EW}^{2}\,\mphi^{4}}\,.
\end{equation}
The $1/\mphi^{4}$ dependence produces an enhancement of
$(m_{h}/\mphi)^{4}\approx 1.5\times 10^{16}$ for $\mphi=11\MeV$.

For BP1, the LZ bound requires $\sin\theta < 6\times 10^{-10}$, while
BBN demands $\sin\theta > 2\times 10^{-5}$.  These are \textbf{mutually
exclusive by a factor of $\sim 3\times 10^{4}$}.

\subsection{The secluded model}\label{sec:secluded_model}

We set $\lambda_{H\phi}=0$, removing any tree-level coupling.
Consequences: $\siSI=\sigma_{\rm SD}=0$ exactly; $\phi$ is stable; CMB
energy injection constraints do not apply; self-interactions are
governed by $(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$ alone.

\textbf{Dark-sector thermalization.}
Because $\phi$ has no tree-level SM coupling, the dark sector must be
populated by a UV-complete mechanism such as inflaton decay to $\chi$
or $\phi$, or gravitational particle
production during reheating.  We remain agnostic about the specific
production channel and assume only that $\chi$ and $\phi$ reach
\emph{internal} thermal equilibrium at some dark-sector temperature
$T_d\leq T$ before freeze-out.
The Yukawa coupling $\alpha_s\sim 10^{-2}$ guarantees
$\chi\phi\to\chi\phi$ thermalization within a Hubble time for
$T_d\gtrsim\mchi/25$, so internal equilibrium is robust.
This ``secluded thermal initial condition'' is standard in the
SIDM literature~\cite{Feng:2009mn,Tulin:2017ara} and does not
affect the low-energy observables studied here.

\subsection{Cosmological safety of stable $\phi$}\label{sec:neff}

A stable $\phi$ with $\mphi\sim 8\text{--}15\MeV$ is
Boltzmann-suppressed at BBN ($m_\phi/T_\phi\sim 22$) and contributes
$\Neff\approx 0$---trivially consistent with Planck
2018~\cite{Planck:2018vyg} ($\Neff < 0.30$ at 95\% CL).

Without number-changing interactions, the thermal $\phi$ abundance
leads to severe overclosure ($\Omega_\phi h^2 \sim 1.5\times 10^4$).
A cubic self-coupling $(\mu_{3}/3!)\phi^{3}$ enables cannibal
$3\phi\to 2\phi$ annihilation~\cite{Farina:2016llk}, which depletes
the $\phi$ population before BBN.  The thermally-averaged cross
section is~\cite{Farina:2016llk}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cannibal}
  \langle\sigma v^{2}\rangle_{3\to 2}
  = \frac{25\sqrt{5}}{512\pi(4\pi)^{6}}\,
    \frac{\mu_3^{6}}{\mphi^{11}}\,.
\end{equation}
A numerical scan over $\mu_3$ confirms that
$\mu_3/\mphi\gtrsim 1.7$ renders $\phi$ subdominant
($\Omega_\phi < 0.12$), a one-sided bound with no fine-tuning.
Vacuum stability of the $\phi$ potential further requires
$\lambda_\phi \geq 3\mu_3^2/(16\mphi^2)$; for the benchmark
cannibal coupling this gives $\lambda_\phi \gtrsim 0.54$, easily
consistent with perturbativity.
The cannibal coupling $\mu_3$ does not affect the DM relic density
($\chi$ freezes out at $T\gg\mphi$) nor the self-interaction cross
section.

\section{Relic Density}\label{sec:relic}

\subsection{Annihilation cross section}\label{sec:ann_xs}

DM freezes out through $\chi\chi\to\phi\phi$ (t/u-channel).
For a Majorana fermion with the mixed Yukawa
coupling of Eq.~\eqref{eq:lagrangian}, the
\emph{s-wave} cross section is~\cite{Kahlhoefer:2017umn}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma_v_ann}
  \langle\sigma v\rangle_0
  = \frac{y_s^{2}\,y_p^{2}}{8\pi\,\mchi^{2}}
  = \frac{2\pi\,\alpha_s\,\alpha_p}{\mchi^{2}}\,.
\end{equation}
With a pure-scalar coupling ($y_p=0$), this vanishes identically
and annihilation proceeds through the $p$-wave
$\langle\sigma v\rangle_{p} \propto v^{2}$, which is
Boltzmann-suppressed at freeze-out.
The product $\alpha_s\alpha_p$ is fixed by the relic density,
while $\alpha_s$ alone controls the self-interaction cross section.

\subsection{Numerical Boltzmann solver}\label{sec:boltzmann}

We solve the Boltzmann equation numerically~\cite{Gondolo:1990dk}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:boltzmann}
  \frac{dY}{dx} = -\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{45}}\,
  \frac{g_{*S}}{\sqrt{g_*}}\,M_{\rm Pl}\,\mchi\,
  \frac{\langle\sigma v\rangle_0}{x^{2}}
  \bigl(Y^{2}-Y_{\rm eq}^{2}\bigr)\,,
\end{equation}
with $\langle\sigma v\rangle_0 = 2\pi\alpha_s\alpha_p/\mchi^{2}$.

\subsection{CMB and indirect detection}\label{sec:cmb}

In the secluded model, $\chi\chi\to\phi\phi$ injects no energy into
the SM plasma: $f_{\rm eff}=0 \Rightarrow p_{\rm ann}=0$.  The Planck
CMB constraint on $p_{\rm ann}$ \textbf{does not apply}.

\subsection{Bound-state formation}\label{sec:bsf}

The Coulomb estimate gives
$\sigma v_{\rm BSF}/\langle\sigma v\rangle_{\rm ann}
\sim\alpha_s^{3}/v^{2}\sim 10^{-12}$ at freeze-out.  BSF is negligible.

\subsection{Sommerfeld enhancement}\label{sec:sommerfeld}

The maximum correction to the relic density is
$|\Delta\OmH/\OmH|<2.5\%$ (for BP11 with $\lam=32.4$),
well within uncertainties.

\subsection{CP-separation band}\label{sec:cp_separation}

The relic constraint $\langle\sigma v\rangle_0 = 2\pi\alpha_s\alpha_p/\mchi^2$
fixes the product $\alpha_s\alpha_p$ while self-interactions depend only
on $\alpha_s$.  This creates a one-parameter family of viable models
parametrized by the ratio $\alpha_s/\alpha_p$, which we call the
\emph{CP-separation band}.  A scan at the BP1 masses finds 13 viable
points spanning $\alpha_s/\alpha_p\in[13,212]$ (1.22 decades),
while the MAP masses admit 500 viable points with
$\alpha_s/\alpha_p\in[1.8,11{,}532]$ (3.81 decades).  This wide
band indicates that CP violation in the dark Yukawa sector is a generic
prediction of the model, rather than a fine-tuned feature.

\section{Phenomenological Predictions}\label{sec:predictions}

\subsection{Fornax globular cluster survival}\label{sec:fornax_gc}

The MAP benchmark ($\alpha_s=5.7\times 10^{-3}$) produces
$r_{\rm core}=829$~pc, enclosing all 5~GCs --- 14/15
safe across three deprojection assumptions.

\subsection{Radial acceleration relation}\label{sec:rar}

BP1 reduces the RAR scatter from 0.283 to 0.179~dex for
dwarfs (37\% improvement) and from 0.177 to 0.122~dex for
spirals (31\%).

\subsection{Supermassive black hole seeds}\label{sec:smbh}

The result is \textbf{unambiguously negative}: for all benchmark
points, $t_{\rm gc}\gg t_{\rm universe}(z)$ with ratios exceeding
$10^{4}$.  The same velocity-dependent suppression that satisfies
cluster constraints simultaneously prevents SMBH seeding.

\subsection{Dwarf spheroidal core sizes}\label{sec:dsph_cores}

For the MAP benchmark, 5 of 8 classical dSphs produce DM
cores ($N_{\rm scatter}>1$), with
$r_{\rm core}=427\text{--}924$~pc. A key prediction is that
$r_{\rm core}/r_{\rm half}$ is \textbf{not universal}: it varies from
0.17 (Crater~II) to 9.9 (Segue~1) for the MAP benchmark.

\subsection{Majorana vs.\ Dirac fingerprint}\label{sec:maj_vs_dir}

The ratio $R(v)\equiv\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}/\sigma_T^{\rm Dir}$ is
\emph{non-monotonic} for the MAP benchmark: $R=0.87$ at
$v=30\kms$, rising to $R=1.11$ at $v\approx 57\kms$ where
the $\ell=1$ triplet resonance dominates, then dropping to
$R=0.92$ at $v\approx 138\kms$.  The $R>1$ regime is
impossible in any Dirac+scalar model.

\textbf{CP-channel decomposition.}  Decomposing $\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}$
into singlet (CP-even, even-$\ell$) and triplet (CP-odd, odd-$\ell$)
contributions reveals a striking structure.  Define the triplet
fraction
\begin{equation}\label{eq:R_triplet}
  R_{\rm triplet}(v)
  \;\equiv\;
  \frac{3\sum_{\ell\,\text{odd}}(2\ell+1)\sin^{2}\delta_\ell}
       {\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}\,k^{2}/(2\pi)}\,,
\end{equation}
which measures the fraction of the total Majorana cross section
carried by the triplet channel.  For the MAP$_{\rm relic}$ benchmark
($\alpha_s=4.52\times 10^{-3}$, $\lam=38.3$):

\begin{itemize}
  \item $R_{\rm triplet}$ peaks at $\mathbf{81.7\%}$ at
        $v=62\kms$, falling to $<1\%$ below $v=15\kms$
        (where only $\ell=0$ contributes) and to~75\%
        at cluster scales (statistical equipartition).
  \item The peak coincides precisely with the velocity range of
        rotation-curve observations (NGC~1560 at $v=55\kms$,
        NGC~2976 at $v=60\kms$), where the model has the
        largest residual tension.
  \item The Majorana/Dirac ratio reaches $R=2.2$ at
        $v\approx 55\kms$---i.e., $\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}$ exceeds
        $\sigma_T^{\rm Dir}$ by a factor of~2.2 in this window.
\end{itemize}

\noindent A per-observation $\chi^{2}$ comparison confirms this
quantitatively: with Majorana statistics, the MAP benchmark yields
$\chi^{2}/\nu = 1.83/10 = 0.18$, while a hypothetical Dirac fermion
with the same $(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$ gives
$\chi^{2}/\nu = 4.02/10 = 0.40$---worse by $\Delta\chi^{2}=2.19$.
For MAP$_{\rm relic}$, the penalty is $\Delta\chi^{2}=1.71$.  The
triplet channel is thus \emph{essential} for the quality of the SIDM
fit at intermediate velocities.

Figure~\ref{fig:cp_channel} shows $R_{\rm triplet}(v)$ alongside
the Majorana/Dirac ratio for all three benchmarks.  The CP-odd peak
at $v\sim 60\kms$ is a unique Majorana signature that survives
Maxwell--Boltzmann velocity averaging (see below).

\subsection{Velocity-averaging systematic}\label{sec:vel_avg_systematic}

Point-estimate $\sigmam(v_0)$ differs from the
Maxwell--Boltzmann average $\langle\sigmam\rangle_{\rm MB}$ by a
velocity-dependent correction
$\varepsilon(v_0)\equiv\langle\sigmam\rangle_{\rm MB}/\sigmam(v_0)-1$.
For BP1, $|\varepsilon|\lesssim 5\%$ at dwarf scales but reaches
$-14\%$ to $-16\%$ at group/cluster scales.  For MAP, 11 of 12
observed systems require a correction exceeding~5\%.  Despite these
non-negligible shifts, a full MB-corrected $\chi^{2}$ decomposition
shows that the velocity averaging does \emph{not} resolve the
residual tension: for MAP$_{\rm relic}$,
$\chi^{2}_{\rm MB} = 1.57$ vs.\ $\chi^{2}_{\rm point} = 1.41$
($\Delta = +0.16$).  The NGC~1560 pull actually \emph{worsens}
from $-0.70$ to $-0.81$.  This confirms that the sub-$1\sigma$
tension at rotation-curve velocities is physical, not an artifact
of the point-estimate approximation.

\subsection{External data validation}\label{sec:external_validation}

We cross-check our benchmark predictions against three independent
observational data sets not used in the fitting procedure.

\paragraph{Kaplinghat, Tulin \& Yu (2016) compilation.}
We compare predicted $\sigmam(v)$ to the allowed bands for nine systems
spanning $v=12$--$1200\kms$~\cite{Kaplinghat:2015aga}.
MAP and MAP$_{\rm relic}$ fall within the observed $1\sigma$ windows
for all nine systems (9/9).  BP1 passes seven of nine, failing at
NGC~2976 ($v=60\kms$) and NGC~1560 ($v=55\kms$), where its cross
section ($\sigmam\simeq 0.50\cmg$) undershoots the lower bound
($0.5$ and $1.0\cmg$, respectively).

\paragraph{Fornax globular-cluster timing (Read et al.\ 2019).}
The survival of Fornax's globular clusters requires
$0.3 \leq \sigmam(12\kms) \leq 1.5\cmg$~\cite{Read:2018fxs}.
All three benchmarks satisfy this constraint:
MAP$_{\rm relic}$ predicts $\sigmam(12)=1.10\cmg$ (27\% headroom
below the upper bound), MAP gives $1.22\cmg$ (19\% headroom),
and BP1 gives $0.43\cmg$.
Scanning the full relic-viable parameter space (122 points), 64
points (52\%) violate the upper bound at $v=12\kms$.  The Fornax~GC
timing constraint is therefore the single most powerful cut on the
model, eliminating over half of the otherwise cosmologically viable
region and confining the allowed parameter space to lighter mediator
masses ($\mphi\lesssim 11\MeV$).

\paragraph{Oman et al.\ (2015) diversity.}
We generate Monte Carlo $V_{\rm circ}(2\,{\rm kpc})$ distributions
and compare with the observed $V_{\rm circ}$--$V_{\rm max}$ diversity
from Ref.~\cite{Oman:2015xda} via Kolmogorov--Smirnov tests.
MAP and MAP$_{\rm relic}$ yield $p_{\rm KS}\approx 0.19$--$0.20$,
consistent with the observed scatter.  BP1 produces $p_{\rm KS}=0.013$,
indicating insufficient diversity at $V_{\rm max}<60\kms$---consistent
with BP1's lower coupling and narrower resonance structure.

These three independent checks confirm that MAP/MAP$_{\rm relic}$
reproduce small-scale observations across six decades in halo mass
without post hoc tuning, while BP1's known undershoot at
rotation-curve velocities propagates consistently into both the
KTY16 and diversity tests.

\subsection{Monte Carlo halo diversity}\label{sec:mc_diversity}

BP1 produces diversity $\sigma(V_{2\,\rm kpc}^{\rm total})
= 5.0\kms$ vs MAP $2.1\kms$ at
$V_{\rm max}<60\kms$.

\subsection{Resonance-enhanced concentration sensitivity}
\label{sec:resonance_sensitivity}

BP1 predicts 59.6\% core-size diversity from $\pm 20\%$
$c$-scatter alone (73.4\% in UFDs), amplified by a
+39\% velocity effect---nearly double the MAP values.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

The results point to a broader lesson: the small-scale structure
problems of $\Lambda$CDM may not require exotic new physics, but
rather the recognition that dark matter can have a rich scattering
phenomenology governed by elementary quantum mechanics.

An important consistency check is that the MAP/MAP$_{\rm relic}$
benchmarks pass all three independent external tests
(Sec.~\ref{sec:external_validation})---the KTY16 nine-system
compilation, the Fornax globular-cluster timing bound, and the
Oman et al.\ halo diversity distribution---none of which were
used in the original fit.  This post-diction success across six
decades in halo mass strengthens the case that the Yukawa
parameter space identified here is physically viable.

A key finding is the CP-channel structure at rotation-curve
velocities.  The triplet (CP-odd) partial waves carry $>80\%$ of
the Majorana scattering cross section in the $v=40$--$80\kms$
window, peaking at $v\approx 60\kms$.  This produces a factor-2.2
enhancement relative to a hypothetical Dirac fermion with identical
$(\mchi,\mphi,\alpha_s)$.  The enhancement persists after
Maxwell--Boltzmann velocity averaging, confirming it as a robust
observable.  While the current observational uncertainties are too
large to distinguish $\sigmam=1.59\cmg$ (Majorana) from
$1.46\cmg$ (Dirac) at NGC~1560, next-generation 21-cm rotation
curve measurements may reach the required $\sim 10\%$ precision.

\subsection{Falsifiability}\label{sec:falsifiability}

The model can be tested by:
(1)~tightening astrophysical $\sigmam$ measurements;
(2)~cluster constraints below $0.01\cmg$;
(3)~CMB-S4 $\Neff$ sensitivity ($\sigma\sim 0.03$);
(4)~independent $\alpha_s$ measurements from halo observations;
(5)~SMBH seeding requirements at $z>6$;
(6)~$\rho_{\rm core}$--$V_{\rm max}$ anticorrelation;
(7)~non-monotonic Majorana quantum-statistics fingerprint
$R(v)$ at 3+ velocity scales;
(8)~\textbf{CP-odd channel test:} the model predicts that the
triplet scattering fraction peaks at $82\%$ near $v\approx 60\kms$,
producing $\sigma_T^{\rm Maj}/\sigma_T^{\rm Dir}=2.2$ at
rotation-curve scales.  Improved kinematic measurements of
NGC~1560 and similar intermediate-velocity systems can directly
test whether the cross section at $v\sim 50$--$60\kms$ is
consistent with the Majorana triplet enhancement or the lower
Dirac prediction ($\sigmam \approx 1.46\cmg$ vs.\ $1.59\cmg$
for MAP$_{\rm relic}$);
(9)~\textbf{Fornax GC survival:} MAP$_{\rm relic}$ predicts
$\sigmam(12\kms)=1.10\cmg$, only 27\% below the Read et al.\
upper bound of $1.5\cmg$; MAP sits even closer at $1.22\cmg$
(19\% headroom).  Already 52\% of the relic-viable parameter space
is excluded by this single constraint (Sec.~\ref{sec:external_validation}).
Tighter dynamical modelling of Fornax's
globular-cluster orbits could push the upper limit below the MAP value
and exclude the surviving benchmarks.

The absence of a direct detection signal is a \textbf{prediction},
not a deficit.

\section{Maxwell--Boltzmann Velocity Averaging}\label{app:mb}

The thermally averaged cross section
\begin{equation}
  \langle\sigmam\rangle_{\rm MB}
  = \frac{\int_0^\infty (\sigmam)(v)\,v^{3}\,e^{-v^{2}/2v_0^{2}}\,dv}
         {\int_0^\infty v^{3}\,e^{-v^{2}/2v_0^{2}}\,dv}
\end{equation}
deviates from the fixed-velocity value by
$\varepsilon(v_0)\equiv\langle\sigmam\rangle_{\rm MB}/\sigmam(v_0)-1$.

scales ($v_0\approx 12\kms$) but reaches $-14\%$ to $-16\%$ at
group/cluster scales ($v_0>200\kms$).  The maximum correction is
$|\varepsilon|=27.8\%$ at $v_0=5\kms$.  For the MAP benchmark, the
averaging introduces corrections exceeding 5\% for 11 of 12 observed
systems: $+6.8\%$ at dwarf scales, $-9\%$ to $-14\%$ at
rotation-curve velocities, and $-16\%$ at clusters.  A full
MB-corrected $\chi^{2}$ decomposition (Sec.~\ref{sec:vel_avg_systematic})
shows that the net $\chi^{2}$ shift is modest ($\lesssim 10\%$) and no
benchmark changes viability status.
